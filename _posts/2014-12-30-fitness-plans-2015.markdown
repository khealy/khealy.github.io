As so many people do this time of year, I'm thinking about my goals for next year - and how I'll achieve them. Fitness is one of the areas I'm planning to work on.

I feel like I've lost some ground in the past year, maybe because I didn't have any goals to work toward. So my first step will be to register for a race!

However, I realize endurance is only one component of fitness. To be truly fit, I'll also need to work on:

* strength
* flexibility
* speed, and
* balance

So, how do I do that? My plan is to:

* Create a training schedule that includes both speed work and distance runs (endurance + speed) to prepare for the aforementioned race.
* Do light stretches every morning, and stretch after every run (flexibility).
* Do yoga once a week (flexibility + balance).
* Attend "Core" class, which incorporates bodyweight exercises, light weights, and band exercises (strength) twice per week.

Tomorrow I'll search for a local race.
